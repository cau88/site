Upper Valley Homelessness Workshop
Nelson A. Rockefeller Center for Public Policy, Hanover, NH
As a part of this workshop, my group did research on how homelessness manifests in the Upper Valley community. We then spoke to homeless members of the Upper Valley community in a casual setting. With knowledge gained from our research, coupled with the personal stories of the individuals that we met, my group made systematic recommendations on how to address homelessness in our community through a social lens.

National History Day
For five years, from 2009-2013, I participated in the National History Day (NHD) competition and created 5 documentaries to match the varying themes announced annually. Creating my documentaries required conducting various personal interviews, collecting numerous primary & secondary sources, synthesizing the information, highlighting the present day significance, and combining everything together with iMovie or Final Cut Pro. One thing I especially enjoyed about NHD was the freedom the students got to explore topics of interest.
The five topics I researched were: Marie Curie (2009), The Compiler (2010), Equality in Education (2011), Television (2012) & The Paris Riots of 1968 (2013).
