Mobile Apps

ExerciseU
For the 2013 Technovation Challenge, the 5 member team that I led created, did market analysis, and pitched our app ExerciseU, which won Honorable Mention (top 20) status in the international competition. Our team was later invited to the 2013 AppInventor Summit at MIT to present our work. ExerciseU was created to combat a sedentary lifestyle for people with busy schedules (ex. college students, busy mothers, etc.) My role on the team consisted of completely programming the app in AppInventor and doing market research. Below you can find screenshots of the app.



TutorEd
For the 2014 Technovation Challenge, the 5 member team that I led created, did market analysis, and pitched our app TutorEd. TutorEd was created to make setting up students and tutors easier, through an almost tutor social network. My role on the team consisted of completely programming the app in AppInventor, and designing the aesthetics. Below you can find a video of the app at work.




Motley Mouse
As part of the 2015 24-hour Dartmouth Hackathon ( HackDartmouth ), the 4 member team that I was on created an app called Motley Mouse which tailored tweets (academic, job/internship-hunting resources, etc.) for specific demographics (students, teachers, parents). My role on the team was coding the whole app with Java, the Twitter API and Android Studio.


Web Apps

During the summer of 2017, I interned at Verizon Communications, Inc. as a Systems Engineering intern on the Voice over LTE (VoLTE) team. A major task that I worked on was the creation of an automated web application that administered health checks. I used jQuery, AJAX, and PHP to accomplish this task.
