National History Day
For five years, from 2009-2013, I participated in the National History Day (NHD) competition and created 5 documentaries to match the varying themes announced annually. Creating my documentaries required conducting various personal interviews, collecting numerous primary & secondary sources, synthesizing the information, highlighting the present day significance, and combining everything together with iMovie or Final Cut Pro. One thing I especially enjoyed about NHD was the freedom the students got to explore topics of interest. The five topics I researched were: Marie Curie (2009), The Compiler (2010), Equality in Education (2011), Television (2012) & The Paris Riots of 1968 (2013).

Moody's Mega Math Challenge
During my junior year of high school (2013) I participated in the national mathematical modeling contest, Moody's Mega Math Challenge, sponsored by The Moody's Foundation and the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM)."
2013 Theme: "Waste Not, Want Not: Putting Recyclables in Their Place"

3 Main Tasks:
  1. Create a model for the amount of plastic that ends up in landfills in the United States and extrapolate over the next 10 years.
  2. Make a local model and recommend different recycling methods that your selected city should adopt.
  3. After applying your local model to a variety of cities, apply your model on a national scale. Inform and recommend the EPA about national recycling standards.

Alzheimer's Research Project
Due to personal reasons, I had been and still am interested in Alzheimer's disease. When I began doing some preliminary research and realized that Alzheimer's could not be prevented, cured, or even slowed, that is when I decided that I wanted to do a research project on it; specifically focusing on if any progress has been made in any of those three areas (prevention, development of a cure, or attenuation).
I contacted three specialists (Dr. Jaya Padmanabhan of the Univerity of South Florida (USF), Dr. George Bloom of the University of Virginia (UVa), & Dr. Jordan Tang of the Oklahoma Medical Research Foundation (OMRF)). I am grateful to the two specialists who replied back (Dr. Bloom & Dr. Tang) and gave me research articles to further my work. My culminating slideshow presentation can be found below.
