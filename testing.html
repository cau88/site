Computer Science Advocacy

I believe there is a lot of power in Computer Science (CS). With CS you can solve problems, create academic resources, build your own games, and change your world. For this reason I advocate for making CS accessible to more and a greater diversity of people, as well as support groups with similar missions and goals. I have been a volunteer for the Connecticut Computer Science Teachers Association (CTCSTA) since 2009, and I have recently joined the App Inventor Brain Trust (2016).

CTCSTA
  Nov. 20, 2015 - Represented the CTCSTA in collaboration with Connecticut State Department of Education (CSDE) at the
    2015 CABE/CAPSS Convention. We advocated for the Hour of Code to take place in every CT school to help all students
     gain access to computer science education and resources.
    *CABE/CAPSS - Connecticut Association of Boards of Education/Connecticut Association of Public School Superintendents

Brain Trust
  Technovation in Boston is creating an App Inventor helpine from Technovation alumni and others with App Inventor
  knowledge. Members of the hotline will serve as resources for the Technovation teams by equipping them with App Inventor
  resources and references. Helpline members do not help the teams create any part of their product, but are a resource for the
  teams if they have questions. I am enthusiastic about being able to help other girls explore tech and entreprenuership,
  especially because of the positive effect Technovation had on me personally.

Other
  Dec. 13, 2014 - Delivered the welcome presentation & was a panelist at the Hartford based Women in STEM-C event. The Women in STEM-C event gives middle and high school girls opportunities to interact with STEM and Computer Sciece activities, in hopes of peaking their interest.
  Apr. 4, 2014 - Presenter at Pfizer BT4G event. Pfizer hosts an annual event where they encourage girls to explore technology. I presented at this event in 2014 about my experience as an NCWIT National Award Winner and the opportunities available to and accomplishments attributed to Women in Tech. I was not able to attend the 2015 event because I was at college.

I am highly passionate about advocacy for females and underrepresented minorities in technology. If you would like me to attend or speak at an advocacy event feel free to contact me at cuche.18@dartmouth.edu with the words "CS Advocacy" in the subject line.
